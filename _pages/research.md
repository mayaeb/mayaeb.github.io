My research investigates how metabolism influences cell fate decisions through integrated effects on chromatin structure and nuclear mechanics.

## Polyamines and Cell Fate

Polyamines are polycationic metabolites abundant in proliferating stem and cancer cells. My work demonstrates that polyamine levels decrease during differentiation and that inhibiting polyamine synthesis drives cells toward a differentiated state. Mechanistically, polyamines interact electrostatically with DNA on the nucleosome core, freeing histone tails to conformations accessible to chromatin-modifying enzymes.

## Metabolism and Nuclear Mechanics

My current work explores an unexpected connection between polyamines and nuclear mechanics. I am investigating how polyamines regulate nuclear envelope tension through osmotic effects and whether cells modulate these mechanical forces during differentiation to drive fate transitions.

This integrated approach connects metabolism, chromatin biology, and cellular mechanics, with implications for understanding development, tissue homeostasis, cancer, and aging.
